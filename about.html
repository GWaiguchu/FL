About Me

I’m Fredrick Lala, a wildlife biologist working in Tsavo National Park, one of Africa’s most iconic ecosystems. With a background in Wildlife Management, my work focuses on the intersection of human infrastructure and wildlife behavior, ensuring a sustainable future for both.

Biography

Born and raised in Nakuru, Kenya, I developed a passion for wildlife conservation at an early age. Over the years, my research has explored the impact of infrastructure on species like lions, elephants, and Critically Endangered Hirola. I also have a keen interest in studying wildlife bones, which provide valuable insights into ecosystem health.
